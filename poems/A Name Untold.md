Long I've wandered, seeking a name unknown,  
An identity, a moniker, a title to intone.  
An exile, a knight radiant, a keeper of hearths,  
A tender of gardens, a pilgrim of many paths.

I've walked on sunlit mountain tops,  
I've rested in the depths, where light stops.  
Wearing many guises, no fixed costume,  
Travailing the soul's castle, known in every room.

Something yet escapes my hold,  
To know my true kind, a name untold.  
What word could compass both light and dark,  
Earth and sky, and marry contrasts stark?

Chthonic depths and airy heights,  
Where reason grounds and vision flights. 
Abstract yet embodied, rooted but upright,  
A righteous oak, humbled might.

Branches like leafy tusks give shade,  
Not alone, but one among the green glade.  
A rooted wanderer, eyes on heaven's brim,  
I am given my name, the Oaken Pilgrim.
